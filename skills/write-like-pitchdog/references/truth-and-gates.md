# Truth and gates

## Contents

- Authority and claim classification
- High-risk claims and permissions
- Protected wording and publication exclusions
- Final truth check

Voice never outranks truth.

## Authority order

Use the highest available source:

1. The user's current explicit instruction.
2. Current owner-approved wording and dated facts.
3. Protected exact wording in the current canon.
4. Current visible approved copy.
5. Earlier approved source material that has not been superseded.
6. New writing where the earlier material is weak or missing.

Legacy drafts are provenance, not permission to restore old claims.

## Classify before writing

### Verified fact

Use only within its stated scope and date. Preserve exact names, pronouns,
amounts, units, and qualifiers.

### User-provided fact

Treat as supplied, not independently verified. Do not strengthen it into a
broader claim.

### Third-party quote

Keep verbatim. Do not “improve” grammar or adapt it into pitch.dog voice.
Publication also requires permission for the exact wording, attribution, and
media pairing.

### Unknown or unresolved fact

Do not infer it. In an internal draft, use a visible gate such as:

`[PLACEHOLDER: OWNER — EXACT FACT OR WORDING NEEDED]`

In a reader-ready draft, omit the claim or state the blocker separately. Never
let a plausible sentence disguise missing evidence.

### Joke or self-description

A clearly non-reliance-bearing observation may be playful. It cannot change a
price, deadline, entitlement, licence, privacy promise, or other term.

## Claims requiring special care

### Outcomes and recognition

- Never guarantee a green light, shortlist, bid, investment, sale, response, or
  other decision.
- Distinguish work made for a recognised project from endorsement by an
  institution.
- Attribute recognition to the correct project and people.

### Pricing and schedules

- A planning estimate is not a quote.
- State the source currency, snapshot date, tax status, and proposal authority
  when relevant.
- Describe ranges as typical only when currently approved.
- Availability and rush work depend on the live queue and real review cut-offs.

### Scope, revisions, and handover

- Keep generosity tied to agreed scope.
- Name deliverable formats and exclusions.
- Do not promise rights in fonts, stock, archives, software, or other
  third-party material that pitch.dog cannot transfer.

### Client work and confidentiality

- Private by default.
- Portfolio use, testimonials, case studies, public teaching edits, crops,
  outcomes, and social posts need permission for the exact use.
- Permission for one asset or quote does not grant permission for the whole
  project.
- Do not use confidential client material as a voice example.

### Identity and politics

- Preserve names and pronouns exactly.
- Do not claim an identity on someone's behalf.
- Treat chosen family as family.
- Avoid default male founder, client, director, or creative-genius language.
- Show feminism, queer dignity, and solidarity through labour, consent, credit,
  access, and power rather than campaign decoration.

### Programmes and offers

- Reverify open or closed state, cadence, eligibility, response timing, form
  destination, and capacity before publication.
- Keep programme consent separate from newsletter consent.
- Free help must not conceal a sales funnel.

## Protected wording

- Use a protected line only in its approved context.
- Keep it exact unless the owner explicitly changes it.
- Do not treat protected wording as a reusable template for unrelated copy.
- Do not multiply one protected joke across several surfaces.
- If current canon and remembered wording conflict, stop and retrieve the
  current canon.

## Publication exclusions

Do not place these in a public skill corpus or public-facing draft:

- client testimonials used as voice training;
- sensitive personal history without explicit approval;
- private client names, assets, results, or correspondence;
- stale prices, reach figures, programme states, or capacity claims;
- placeholders, internal approval notes, or legal drafts awaiting review.

## Final truth check

Before delivery:

- verify names, pronouns, dates, money, units, and links;
- separate fact from inference and judgement;
- remove guarantees the evidence cannot support;
- check quote and media permission;
- check confidentiality and ownership;
- check current programme and commercial state;
- leave unresolved facts visibly gated or omitted;
- confirm the joke cannot be mistaken for a real term.
